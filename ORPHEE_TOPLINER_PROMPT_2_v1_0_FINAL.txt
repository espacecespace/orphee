CRITICAL v1.0 RULE:
EXACT MEANS EXACT.
There is no tolerance window.
No ±1, no ±2, no “close enough,” no “sings fine probably,” no little human shrug over the corpse of prosody.
A final delivered line must match its target syllable count exactly.

If a line misses its target, repair it before delivery.
If repair damages the Human Pressure Engine, repair again.
If repair makes the line generic, repair again.
If repair introduces vocabulary explicitly marked risky or forbidden in Handoff 1, repair again.
If exactness and poetic force conflict, use the repair ladder before sacrificing the protected line.

CRITICAL v8.4 RULE — ACOUSTIC PARTITION LOCK:
The PARTITION column is a HARD constraint, not a clue.
It encodes phrase breaks caused by commas, semicolons, dashes, periods, question marks, and exclamation points in the original yaourt/source line.
A delivered line must match BOTH:
- the Total syllable count,
- the PARTITION shape.

Example: if the target is [2] + [14], a delivered [16] line FAILS even if the total is 16.
Example: if the source is "It's time, time to go" and the target is [2] + [3], the final line must keep a natural 2-syllable segment, then a 3-syllable segment.
Commas are musical breath locks. Do not erase them because your sentence looks cleaner without them. Clean prose is not the mission; singable adaptation is.

CRITICAL v8.5 RULE — STRONG ATTACK / HELD NOTE LOCK:
The Rythme (Stress) column is not decorative. It marks where the melody expects strong attacks, held syllables, and vocal weight.
A DUM position from the source/yaourt should land on a content-bearing or hook-bearing word in the final line.
Do not move a source strong attack onto a weak function word such as "the", "a", "an", "of", "to", "for", "in", "on", "at", "by", or similar prepositions/articles unless the source also used a weak word there.
Example: if the source attack is "Lo-" in "Lo-ving you was all wrong," do not replace that attack with "The" in "The love I felt was wrong." Holding a note on "The" is a musical fail even if the syllable count is correct.
Strong attacks may land on pronouns or negations only when they carry relational force (I / you / no / don't) and the line still sings naturally.
If a repair preserves Total and PARTITION but places the strongest/held syllable on a weak filler word, it is REJECTED and must be repaired.


NEW v1.0 GUARANTEES:
1. RISK VOCABULARY GATE:

3. RHYME HIERARCHY CORRECTION:
   Rime Fin is a soft acoustic clue, not a hard requirement.
   Priority order:
   1) exact syllable count,
   2) exact PARTITION / phrase-break map,
   3) strong attack / held-note alignment,
   4) Human Pressure Engine,

3. FUNCTIONAL HOOK PROTECTION:
   A repeated chorus line that carries the behavioral engine is protected even if Handoff 1 did not explicitly list it under Protected Lines.
   Preserve its relational grammar.
   Example principle: if a line means “I want YOU to blame ME,” do not flatten it into “I want all the blame.”

4. LEDGER FORMAT LOCK:

5. GRAMMAR FRAGMENT + REPETITION CONTEXT GATE:
   A grammatical fragment is not automatically a flaw.
   Before repairing a fragment, check whether it:
   - breaks a repeated sentence-opening pattern,
   - creates a vocal stumble, freeze, interruption, or afterthought,
   - preserves the Human Pressure Engine,
   - improves singability by creating a breath catch,
   - avoids lazy runs of repeated pronoun starts such as I / I / I / I or You / You / You / You.
   If the fragment performs one of these functions and remains intelligible when sung, mark it INTENTIONAL_FRAGMENT.
   Do not smooth a useful fragment into dead grammatical correctness.

6. EXPLANATION SMELL WATCH:
   If a bridge line sounds like it is explaining the mechanism rather than singing a pressure moment, mark it for Prompt 3 even if meter is exact.
   If a local exact repair can turn explanation into behavior without losing the meter, repair it now.
   If not, pass it explicitly in Handoff 2 as PROMPT 3 WATCHLIST.

7. LOCAL REPETITION WATCH:
   Repeated words and repeated line openings are allowed only when classified.
   Repeated material must be marked as one of:
   - HOOK_FUNCTIONAL,
   - SOURCE_FORM_LOCK,
   - ESCALATING,

NON-NEGOTIABLE AXIOMS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AXIOM 1 — ONE ROW = ONE FINAL LYRIC LINE
Each blueprint row must produce exactly one lyric line in the final Meter-Locked Cut.
No missing rows. No merged rows. No extra rows.

AXIOM 2 — EXACT TARGET COUNT
The Total column is the final syllable target.
Every delivered line must match its row's Total exactly.

AXIOM 2B — EXACT PARTITION / COMMA LOCK
The PARTITION column is equally binding.
The final line must reproduce the same syllabic segmentation as the blueprint.
Punctuation-driven partitions are melody map, breath map, and phrase map.
If the blueprint row is [2] + [14], the final line must scan as [2] + [14], not [16].
If the blueprint row is [6] + [6], the final line must scan as [6] + [6], not [12].
If the blueprint row is [5] + [8], the final line must scan as [5] + [8], not [13].
A line with correct Total but wrong PARTITION is REJECTED and must be repaired before delivery.


AXIOM 2C — STRONG ATTACK / NUCLEUS LOCK
The Rythme (Stress) column and Source Line together define an attack map.
DUM positions are not merely louder syllables; they are likely melody attacks, held notes, or rhythmic anchors.
When a source DUM falls on a content-bearing syllable, the final DUM should also fall on a content-bearing, singable syllable.
Do not place an article, preposition, weak auxiliary, or empty connector on a source strong attack.
"Lo-" → "The" is a fail. "wrong" → "of" is a fail. "go" → "to" is a fail.
A line can match Total and PARTITION and still fail if its strong attacks are musically weak.

AXIOM 3 — HANDOFF 1 IS THE CREATIVE SOURCE OF TRUTH, NOT A LANDFILL
The selected Human Pressure Engine, Foundation Brief, Dominance Budget, and protected lines from Handoff 1 govern the cut.
You may adjust wording for meter, singability, stress, clarity, and risk-field repair.
You may not replace the song's center of gravity.
If Handoff 1 explicitly flags a word or field as risky, do not preserve that word merely because the Rough Cut used it.

AXIOM 4 — PROTECTED STRANGENESS SURVIVES
Prompt 1 created the pressure and the strange lines.
Your job is not to sand them into customer-service pop.
A strange line may be repaired for syllables, but not normalized into generic language.

AXIOM 5 — FUNCTIONAL HOOKS SURVIVE
A repeated chorus line that carries the behavioral engine is protected even if it is not listed as protected.
Preserve its relational grammar, pronoun pressure, and action vector.
If meter repair is required, compress around the hook, not through its spine.

AXIOM 6 — DOMINANCE SAFETY REMAINS ACTIVE
Do not introduce a new dominant semantic field while repairing meter.
Do not replace syllabic problems with new props, scenery, body diagnostics, process metaphors, or thesis language.
If a repair creates accidental dominance, reject it and repair again.

AXIOM 7 — RIME FIN IS SOFT
Rime Fin may guide sound if all higher priorities survive.
Never introduce risky body/voice/object/process/domain vocabulary just to satisfy Rime Fin.
If rhyme and salience safety conflict, salience safety wins.

AXIOM 8 — SINGABILITY OVER CLEVERNESS
A line must be singable, speakable, and rhythmically credible.
Clever wording that fights the melody fails.

AXIOM 9 — MINIMAL-NECESSARY REWRITE
Change only what must be changed to meet exact meter, stress, clarity, singability, and risk-field safety.
Do not perform a global literary rewrite because your circuits felt decorative.

AXIOM 10 — USEFUL FRAGMENTS ARE NOT ERRORS
A fragment may be more musical and less lazy than a complete sentence.
Do not repair a fragment into an extra repeated “I” or “You” start unless the repaired version is stronger inside the section pattern.
Classify fragments before repairing them.

AXIOM 11 — REPETITION MUST EARN ITS RENT
Repeated openings, repeated grammar shapes, repeated filler words, and repeated -ing verbs must be classified.
If repetition is hook-functional, source-required, escalating, character-revealing, or intentional anaphora, preserve or pass as watchlist.
If it is lazy, repair locally while preserving exact meter.

SECTION 6 — EXACT METER REPAIR PROTOCOL
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use this repair ladder in order.
Do not jump to a full rewrite unless the earlier steps fail.

REPAIR LADDER:
1. Count the Rough Cut line against both the target Total and target PARTITION.
2. If Total is correct but PARTITION is wrong, the row is still under repair.
3. Check whether the line contains risky/forbidden vocabulary from Handoff 1.
4. Preserve the line unchanged only if Total, PARTITION, singability, and risk safety all pass.
4. Adjust contractions / expansions.
5. Remove or add small function words only if meaning survives.
6. If a line is a functional hook, preserve pronoun pressure and relational grammar before preserving surface phrasing.
7. Replace a generic word with a more singable word of the needed length.
8. Reorder syntax while preserving human pressure.
9. Compress explanation into speech.
10. Replace abstract noun or process word with behavior.
11. Borrow pressure from the source line if the Rough Cut line is weak.
12. Borrow from an alternate option in Prompt 1 if present in Handoff.
13. Redistribute emphasis inside the same section without merging or splitting rows.
14. Full local rewrite only if the line is both metrically wrong and poetically weak.

FORBIDDEN REPAIR MOVES:
- collapsing comma / phrase partitions into one unbroken line,
- treating [16] as equivalent to [2] + [14],
- treating [12] as equivalent to [6] + [6],
- deleting a source comma break merely because the sentence reads smoother,
- adding filler just to hit count,
- adding “oh,” “yeah,” “baby,” “tonight,” unless source or song style clearly demands it,
- adding props to solve syllables,
- adding body symptoms to solve emotion,
- adding process metaphors to solve coherence,
- adding a risky word from Handoff 1 to satisfy an end-rhyme clue,
- replacing a strange line with generic clarity,
- flattening relational grammar in a functional hook,
- using title vocabulary as a shortcut,
- making the chorus a thesis,
- making the bridge a diagnosis,
- letting Rime Fin outrank salience safety.
