NON-NEGOTIABLE AXIOMS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AXIOM 1 — HANDOFF 2 IS THE SOURCE OF TRUTH
Prompt 2 has already locked the topline to meter.
You may repair locally, but you may not replace the song’s center of gravity.

AXIOM 2 — ONE ROW = ONE FINAL LYRIC LINE
Every blueprint row must produce exactly one lyric line in the MASTER CUT.
No missing rows. No merged rows. No extra rows.

AXIOM 3 — EXACT TARGET COUNT
The Total column is the final syllable target.
Every delivered MASTER CUT line must match its row’s Total exactly.

AXIOM 4 — PROTECTED STRANGENESS SURVIVES
Protected lines are not raw material for smoothing.
A strange line may be repaired only if it becomes more singable while preserving its strangeness, pressure, and exact count.

AXIOM 5 — FUNCTIONAL HOOKS SURVIVE
Repeated chorus lines that carry the behavioral engine are protected.
Do not replace a behavioral hook with a thesis, summary, or prettier abstract equivalent.

AXIOM 6 — LOCAL REPAIR ONLY
You may repair a line, a pair of adjacent lines, or a repeated hook pattern.
You may not rewrite the whole lyric because your circuits want a dramatic second draft.

AXIOM 7 — SALIENCE SAFETY REMAINS ACTIVE
Do not introduce a new dominant field while finalizing.
No new object-anchor, place-anchor, body-diagnosis, process metaphor, institutional domain, weather system, title noun, source noun, or therapy concept may become the song’s center.

AXIOM 8 — RIME FIN IS SOFT
Rime Fin may guide sound only after exactness, engine, hook, salience, and singability survive.
Never chase rhyme by importing risky vocabulary.

AXIOM 9 — USEFUL FRAGMENTS ARE NOT ERRORS
A fragment may be the most alive line in a section.
Do not repair a fragment into grammatical smoothness if it breaks lazy repetition, creates a breath catch, dramatizes freezing, or preserves the narrator’s pressure.

AXIOM 10 — REPETITION MUST EARN ITS RENT
Repeated line openings, repeated grammar shapes, repeated filler words, and repeated -ing verbs must be classified.
Allowed repetition must be one of:
- HOOK_FUNCTIONAL,
- SOURCE_FORM_LOCK,
- ESCALATING,
- CHARACTER_REVEALING,
- INTENTIONAL_ANAPHORA,
- CONTRAST_SETUP.
If none apply, it is LAZY_REPETITION and must be repaired locally.

AXIOM 11 — FINAL CUT MUST STILL FEEL WRITTEN BY A PERSON
Exactness is not enough.
A final lyric that is metrically exact but emotionally flat fails.
A line that sounds like an explanation, prompt artifact, or audit sentence must be repaired or watchlisted if repair would damage meter.

AXIOM 12 — DIFF HONESTY IS NON-NEGOTIABLE
Every lexical change from Handoff 2 to Master Cut must be declared.
Article changes, pronoun changes, verb tense changes, auxiliary changes, word order changes, and restored earlier lines all count.

AXIOM 13 — NO PERFECT-SCORE AUDIT THEATER
A jury that scores everything 5/5 must run an Overconfidence Audit.
Perfect scores are allowed only if counterevidence is examined and documented.

AXIOM 14 — WATCHLISTS ARE CONSERVATIVE
“None” is allowed only after testing for repeated fields, bridge explanation, pronunciation assumptions, local repairs, protected-line delivery dependence, and residual awkwardness.
